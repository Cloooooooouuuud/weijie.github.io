Research Interest

I am broadly interested in how deep learning models represent and reason about the world. I’m especially curious about self-supervised and generative objectives—contrastive learning, masked prediction, diffusion, and multimodal world models—and how these simple objectives give rise to structured internal representations that support downstream reasoning.

Recently, I’ve been exploring chain-of-thought reasoning, analogy prompting, model interpretability, and how representation structure affects reasoning robustness. Long term, I hope to develop empirical frameworks that connect model behavior with principles from causal inference, continual learning, and cognitive science, ultimately toward building models that are more interpretable, more stable over time, and more aligned with how humans reason.
